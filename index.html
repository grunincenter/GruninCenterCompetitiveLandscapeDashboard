Research Purpose & Scope

This strategic research examines how U.S. law schools engage with social entrepreneurship, impact investing, and sustainable development‚Äîthe three pillars of the Grunin Center's mission. Our goal is to understand the competitive landscape and identify opportunities for the Grunin Center's continued leadership and growth.

We reviewed all 199 ABA-accredited law schools, examining their institutes, centers, clinics, events, and extracurricular offerings. From this comprehensive review, we identified 17 institutions with meaningful programming in our focus areas.

9 Strong Institutional Commitment

Schools demonstrating a high level of commitment through a dedicated center or institute acting as a singular organizing hub, coordinating clinics, courses, student groups, and recurring events. Often involves cross-campus collaboration and visible leadership support.

3 Substantial Institutional Commitment

Schools with clear focus through specific programming, though typically part of a broader general program (e.g., general entrepreneurship center) and/or lacking dedicated institutional commitment. Social enterprise work exists but isn't a stated core mission.

5 Some Institutional Commitment

Schools offering relevant programming as a secondary focus, typically within broader entrepreneurship, sustainability, or community/international development programs. Lacks explicit keyword focus in current mission or name.

Key Finding

The Grunin Center remains the only law school center comprehensively addressing all three pillars‚Äîsocial entrepreneurship, impact investing, AND sustainable development‚Äîunder one institutional umbrella.

Research Methodology

Our research combined systematic manual review with intentional AI assistance at key points in the process. This approach allowed us to comprehensively survey the landscape while ensuring accuracy and depth in our analysis.

Step 1: Foundation Review

Reviewed the 2022 strategic planning slide deck and visited the websites of programs referenced in that document to establish baseline knowledge and identify previously tracked institutions.

Step 2: Comprehensive School List

Pulled a list of all 199 ABA-accredited United States law schools to ensure complete coverage of the competitive landscape.

Step 3: Keyword & Prompt Development

Developed a set of keywords and AI prompts to search for programs and activities across all 199 schools. AI helped identify search terms and variations that would surface relevant programming.

Step 4: Structured Website Research

Visited the website of all 199 law schools and conducted structured searches. Research scope covered the past three academic years: 2023-24, 2024-25, and 2025-26.

Step 5: AI-Assisted Discovery

Used AI to surface programs that manual searches might have missed, ensuring comprehensive coverage and reducing the risk of overlooking relevant offerings.

Step 6: Database Construction

Built a spreadsheet with the following tabs and populated each with information from our searches: T14, Institutes/Centers/Programs, Courses, Events, Teaching Materials, Clinics, CLE, Reports/White Papers, and Extracurriculars.

Step 7: Literature Cross-Check

Cross-checked our database against scholarship from the 2017-2024 literature review to ensure we included colloquia where relevant scholarship was presented, and we looked into programming at schools that had a prolific scholar.

Step 8: Short List Development

Analyzed the collected data to create a short list of competitive law schools that show institutional commitment to social entrepreneurship, impact investing, and sustainable development.

Step 9: Categorization

Grouped the schools on the short list into strong, substantial, or some institutional commitment and developed definitions for each category. Used AI to help select category descriptors that would make the most sense for readers.

Step 10: Interdisciplinary Partnerships

Used AI to surface interdisciplinary partnerships connected to social entrepreneurship, impact investing, and sustainable development for the schools on the short list.

Step 11: Synthesis & Presentation

Used AI to pull together our research into a digestible format, creating this interactive dashboard to present findings to the Advisory Board.

ü§ñ Thoughtful Use of AI

Throughout this research, we used AI intentionally at specific points where it added value: initial keyword development, surfacing programs that manual searches might miss, identifying interdisciplinary partnerships, and synthesizing findings into accessible formats. Human judgment guided all categorization decisions and quality checks, ensuring accuracy while leveraging AI's ability to process large amounts of information efficiently.

What They're Offering: Key Patterns

Analysis of all ABA-accredited programs reveals distinct patterns in how law schools approach social entrepreneurship, impact investing, and sustainable development.

üìç Institutes, Centers & Programs (25)

Dedicated vs. General Focus: Most centers focus broadly on entrepreneurship or business law, with social enterprise as one component. Only the Grunin Center comprehensively addresses SE, II, and SD under one umbrella.

üèõÔ∏è Cross-Campus Collaboration

Several programs leverage university-wide resources: Columbia's Climate School partnership, Michigan's cross-school clinics, and Yale's SOM connection. This amplifies impact beyond the law school.

‚öñÔ∏è Clinic Focus Areas (61)

Social Enterprise: Most common focus, with clinics explicitly stating SE clients. Community Development: Strong secondary category. Impact Investing: Rare‚Äîonly NYU ITC, Michigan ITC, and Case Western Reserve's IDL Lab explicitly serve II clients. ESG: Emerging focus area.

üåç International Transactions

Michigan pioneered the international transactional clinic model in 2008. NYU followed. The Ashoka collaboration (Michigan, Georgetown, GW) demonstrates cross-institutional partnership potential.

üìÖ Event Themes (17)

ESG/Sustainable Finance: Grew rapidly across multiple schools with dedicated symposia. Social Entrepreneurship: UMKC's 10-year Midwest Symposium is the longest-running. SE/II/SD together: Only NYU addresses all three in convenings.

üéì CLE Offerings

Limited CLE offerings identified: UMKC Midwest Symposium, UC Berkeley Law ESG University, UT Austin Master Class, NYU Grunin Center workshops. Significant gap in market for practitioner training.

üë• Extracurriculars (33)

Internships: Columbia CCSI offers 6+ sustainable investment internships. Fellowships: Georgetown and Michigan offer 2-year teaching fellowships. Student Groups: Most schools have general entrepreneurship groups; few focus specifically on social enterprise.

üèÜ Recognition Programs

Student awards (White & Case at NYU) and practitioner prizes (Grunin Prizes) are rare. Most schools lack formal recognition mechanisms for excellence in these fields.
